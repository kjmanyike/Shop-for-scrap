Generation

Process of Generating Electricity

Eskom is a state-owned electricity utility responsible for approximately 95% of the electricity generation. Eskom relies primarily on coal as a primary source of energy. Coal-fired plants produce electricity by burning coal in a boiler to produce steam. The steam produced, under tremendous pressure, flows into a turbine, which spins a generator to create electricity. The steam is then cooled, condensed back into water and returned to the boiler to start the process over. Once electricity is generated, it is transferred to end users by means of a network of power lines traversing the landscapes of South Africa.

Across the value chain of electricity, there are environmental impacts. Waste is produced at the various stages. Solid waste management is a problem in many industries, including in the generation of electricity. Without effective management, an increased volume of solid waste will cause adverse environmental impacts, such as contamination pollution of water sources, damage fragmentation of ecological habitats and deterioration of the ambient air quality. One way to reduce the magnitude and severity of these environmental impacts that are coming from increased waste generation is recycling, which is one of the strategies to achieve a greener and more circular economy. The website awards the opportunity to make recyclable waste accessible to customers as part of an initiative to reduce solid waste disposal on land. Customers can enjoy an improve data capturing and information sharing tool; and gain quick access to material that can be reused, recycled.

The aims:

- Reduce disposal of solid waste on land (landfill sites);
- Facilitate the transition towards a circular economy;
- Create partnerships with the community and other organisations through beneficiation;
- Introducing a quick and effective tool for potential recyclers to access material;
- Improved recording of waste.
